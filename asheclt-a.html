Our Story & Mission

Born from community need. Sustained by community trust. Ashe CLT exists because housing affordability in the High Country isn't just an economic problem — it's a human one.

Why Ashe CLT Exists

Rising home prices are pushing out the people who define Ashe County — the teachers, farmers, healthcare workers, and longtime families whose roots run deep in these mountains. Ashe CLT was formed to make sure those people have a place to stay.

Using the nationally proven community land trust model, we acquire land and hold it permanently for the community — so homes on that land can be sold affordably, now and forever.

How a Community Land Trust Works

A simple, powerful idea: keep land in community hands so homes stay affordable forever.

1

Land is held in trust

Ashe CLT acquires land through donations, purchases, or government transfer and holds it permanently in community trust. It is never sold.

2

Homes sold below market

Because the land is removed from the price, homes can be sold 20–40% below market rate to income-qualified families with a real mortgage.

3

99-year ground lease

The homeowner signs a renewable 99-year ground lease with Ashe CLT. They own the home. We own the land. The lease guarantees future affordability.

4

Equity shared fairly

A resale formula lets families earn a fair return when they sell, while keeping the home affordable for the next buyer. Wealth without displacement.

5

Ongoing stewardship

Ashe CLT provides mortgage counseling, maintenance guidance, and community support so families succeed long-term.

6

Community stays whole

Each home added to the trust is permanently off the speculative market — building neighborhood stability for everyone.

FAQ

Your questions, answered

Do I really own my home in a CLT?
Yes — you own the home with a standard mortgage. Ashe CLT owns the land and leases it to you via a 99-year renewable ground lease. You can renovate, paint, and make it your own just like any homeowner.
How much does a CLT home cost?
CLT homes are typically priced 20–40% below market because you're buying the home, not the land. Exact pricing depends on available units and income. Contact us to learn about current opportunities.
Can I pass my home to my children?
Yes. CLT homes are inheritable. Your heirs can continue living in the home or sell it — with the affordability restriction preserved for the next family.
What if I fall behind on my mortgage?
Lenders cannot foreclose without first notifying Ashe CLT. We have the right to step in and help cure the default. CLT homeowners nationwide have a foreclosure rate under 1%.
Who qualifies to buy a CLT home?
Buyers generally earn 50–80% of Area Median Income, have stable income, and qualify for a mortgage. Preference is given to current Ashe County residents. Contact us for current openings.
How can I support Ashe CLT if I'm not buying a home?
Donate, join as a member, donate land, volunteer, or simply spread the word. Every form of support makes a lasting difference.
